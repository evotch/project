When and how do I use this style guide?

This portion of the Digital Style Guide. It used as an asset when creating written content across all digital channels. Please refer to this guide when determining how to treat terms and style on alaskaair.com. This document also speaks to the treatment of titles, headers, and sub headers; the use of text over imagery; and buttons/CTAs. For all grammatical questions, please refer to the Chicago Manual of Style.

Terms

A

am/pm

To express the time of day in regular text, use the following guidelines:

Example

10:00 am (space after number, lowercase letters, no periods)
11:00 pm (space after number, lowercase letters, no periods)

Abbreviations, monetary

Use the following guidelines when it comes to standard monetary abbreviations on alaskaair.com. Do not use "15K" or "$15k" to abbreviate thousands in distances or dollars. However, it may be used when speaking of Mileage Plan miles.

Example

15,000 miles - $15,000 (USD) - 15K Mileage Plan miles

Account

The feature called My Account encompasses a traveler’s personalized information at alaskaair.com, including their Mileage Plan information. In text, it is acceptable to refer to a traveler’s My Account as their My Account profile. See About My Account link for more background.

Affect vs. effect

Affect is a word meaning "to influence." Effect is a word meaning "a result." Just remember: you affect the effect.

Correct

The effect of the new schedule will not be known until after the March 1 launch. We created the new schedule to positively affect the customer experience.

Age

When noting a person’s age with "years old" in text, use the following formats for different parts of speech:

Correct

Adjective: A 14-year-old passenger can fly unaccompanied. Noun: Service fees are required for passengers ages 8 through 12 years old.

Airfare

Always use the term “airfare” as a single word.

Alaskaair.com

The website is referred to as alaskaair.com. Use this format when referring to the website in print or Web text.

Alaska Airlines and Horizon Air

Alaska Airlines and Horizon Air display a registered trademark ® in the lower right-hand area of the logo. As long as the logo appears with the correct ® symbol, it is not necessary to use the symbol in body copy.
